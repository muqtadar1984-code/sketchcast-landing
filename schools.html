For leadership

Answers, not spreadsheets.

Everything a principal's office asks for on a Monday morning — already computed, already scoped to who's asking.

analytics

The at-risk list writes itself

Whole-school activity, completion and overdue work at a glance. Students who go quiet, slip or fall behind are flagged by rule — coordinators get a named, actionable worklist; principals see clean aggregates by grade.

briefing

Ask the school a question

A briefing assistant that answers from your school's live numbers only — "which Grade 5 students need a call home this week?" — with every briefing recorded in the access log.

teacher support

Each teacher's lessons, assignments and grading backlog next to their classes' progress — framed as support flags, not rankings, so help arrives before burnout does.

A plain-language access page states exactly what every role can see, coordinators get scoped grants (their grades, nothing more), and leadership views land in an audit log.

The timetable

A week that builds itself — and covers itself.

Set your start time, period length and breaks. SketchCast fills the rest, and keeps it standing when someone calls in sick.

auto-generate

Good rules, applied everywhere

The class teacher opens every day, core subjects run daily for every class, enrichment fills the gaps with variety — and no teacher is ever double-booked or pushed past their daily cap.

fully editable

Your judgement wins

Drag to move or swap, lock the cells the generator must never touch, copy or clear a day, undo anything. Period times are derived from your start time and breaks — change one number, the whole day shifts.

absence cover

Absent at 7:02, covered by 7:03

Mark a teacher absent and every lesson gets a cover plan — subject teacher first, then class teacher, then the lightest load, only from staff with room in their day. No cover available? It says so, honestly.

personal weeks

Everyone knows where to be

Every teacher sees their own week and any cover duties from their own login; every student sees their class grid, breaks and times included.

The classroom engine

Your textbooks, taught.

The same engine individual teachers love, at school scale: upload the books your school actually teaches from, and every chapter becomes a complete kit.

full kit

Six pieces, one click

Every generate produces the complete kit together — a narrated video lesson that draws itself, an editable deck, a lesson plan, activities, a worksheet and an exam paper — grounded in the chapter, not the internet.

long chapters

Parts, not marathons

Long chapters split into classroom-sized parts — each with its own video and kit, generated on demand, so a 40-page chapter becomes four teachable lessons.

For parents

The report card, every day.

Parents join by school invitation, already linked to their own children — nothing to set up, nothing extra to see.

progress

Each child, at a glance

Assignments, completion, scores and overdue flags per child — read-only, scoped to their own children and no one else's.

calendar

School dates where families look

School events and class dates, subscribable into Google, Outlook or Apple calendars — with assignment deadlines riding along on the in-app calendar.

invitations

Invited, not registered

The school issues each parent invite with the child link already attached — so access is granted deliberately, never self-claimed.

practical
