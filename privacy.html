Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2 July 2026

SketchCast AI (“SketchCast”, “we”, “us”) turns textbook chapters into narrated lessons, decks, videos, and worksheets for schools, teachers, and their students. Many of our users are children, so we hold ourselves to a simple standard: collect the minimum, use it only to teach, never sell it, never advertise with it, and never track behaviour for any purpose other than showing teachers learning progress.

1. What we collect

Teachers, school admins, and coordinators (adults): name, email address, password (stored hashed by our authentication provider) or Google sign-in identity, and your school affiliation.

Students: student accounts are created by a teacher or school — students never sign themselves up. For each student we store: name, a login ID, a parent or guardian email address (used for account communication only), class enrolment, and learning records — which lessons were assigned, completion status, quiz answers and scores, and any answer files the student submits.

Content: textbook chapters and materials that teachers upload, and the lessons, decks, videos, and worksheets we generate from them.

What we do not collect: we use no advertising or analytics trackers, no behavioural profiling SDKs, and no cookies other than the session cookie that keeps you signed in. We do not collect student phone numbers, addresses, photos, or location.

2. Children’s privacy

  • Student accounts exist only inside a school or teacher’s workspace and are created, named, and managed by that school or teacher — acting with parental consent obtained by the school, as required by applicable law including India’s Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDP Act).
  • Student data is used for exactly one purpose: delivering assigned lessons and showing the student’s own teachers and school how learning is going.
  • We never advertise to students, never sell or share student data for commercial purposes, and never use student data to train AI models.
  • Access to student data inside a school is role-scoped: a teacher sees only their own classes; a coordinator sees only the grades assigned to them; school leadership views of student-level data are recorded in an access log the school admin can review.
  • Parents or guardians may review or request deletion of their child’s data at any time — through the school, or directly at the contact address below.

3. How we use data

  • To run the service: sign-in, generating teaching materials, assigning them, and recording progress and grades for the student’s own teachers and school.
  • To communicate about accounts (e.g. login credentials to a parent’s email).
  • To keep the service secure and debug failures.

We do not use your data for advertising, and we do not sell it to anyone.

4. AI processing

Lessons are generated by sending the uploaded chapter content to Anthropic (Claude) and, for narration audio, the lesson script text to Microsoft (Edge text-to-speech) or — only when a school enables premium voices — ElevenLabs. Student personal data is not sent to any AI provider. These providers process the content to provide the service and are not permitted to use it for advertising.

5. Who processes data for us

  • Supabase — database, authentication, and file storage.
  • Vercel — hosts the web application.
  • Railway — runs the lesson-generation worker.
  • Cloudflare — serves this website.
  • Anthropic, Microsoft, ElevenLabs — AI content and narration, as in §4.
  • Google — optional “Sign in with Google” for adult accounts only.

6. Security

Data is encrypted in transit; passwords are hashed; every database query is scoped by row-level security so users can only reach data their role permits; files are served through short-lived signed links; leadership access to student-level data is audit-logged. No system is perfectly secure, but minimal collection is our first protection: what we don’t hold can’t leak.

7. Retention and deletion

Teachers can delete uploaded books and generated content at any time. When a student is removed, their profile and learning records are deleted with them. To close an account or remove a school’s data entirely, email us — we will complete deletion within 30 days, except where law requires longer retention.

8. Your rights

You may request access to, correction of, or deletion of your personal data — and parents or guardians may do so for their child. Contact us at the address below; if you are part of a school, your school admin can also action most requests directly in the product.

9. Changes

If we change this policy, we will update this page and its date, and notify schools of any material change affecting student data before it takes effect.
