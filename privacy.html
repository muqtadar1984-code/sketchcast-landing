Privacy Policy

Last updated: 12 July 2026 · Effective: 12 July 2026

SketchCast AI (“SketchCast”, “we”, “us”, “our”) turns textbook chapters into narrated lessons, decks, videos, worksheets, and an optional book-grounded AI study assistant, for schools, teachers, parents, and their students. Many of our users are children, so we hold ourselves to a simple standard: collect the minimum, use it only to teach, never sell it, never advertise with it, never use it to train AI models, and never track behaviour for any purpose other than showing teachers and parents learning progress.

This policy explains what we collect, why, who we share it with, how long we keep it, and the rights you have. It applies to sketchcast.app and app.sketchcast.app.

2. Which laws apply to you

SketchCast is available in more than one country, so the protections you can rely on depend on where you and your school are. Wherever you are, the commitments above apply. In addition:

  • India — the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act), including its provisions for children’s data and verifiable parental consent.
  • European Economic Area & United Kingdom — the GDPR / UK GDPR, including the rules on children’s consent and on transfers outside the EEA/UK.
  • United States — where a US school uses SketchCast, the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) — under which the school provides consent on parents’ behalf for the limited educational purpose here — and the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), under which we act as a “school official” with a legitimate educational interest and use student records only as the school directs.
  • California — the CCPA/CPRA. We do not “sell” or “share” personal information as those terms are defined, and we honour the additional protections for consumers under 16.
  • Malaysia — the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA).

If a mandatory local law gives you stronger rights than this policy describes, that law prevails.

3. What we collect

Teachers, parents, school admins, and coordinators (adults)

Name, email address, password (stored only as a salted hash by our authentication provider) or Google sign-in identity, role, and — for school staff — school affiliation. At sign-up we also collect a short profile you provide (e.g. the grades/subjects you teach, or, for a parent, how many children you support and their grade levels).

Students

Student accounts are created by a teacher, parent, or school — students never sign themselves up. For each student we store: name, a login ID, a parent/guardian email address (used for account communication only), class enrolment, and learning records — which lessons were assigned, completion status, quiz/worksheet answers and scores, any answer files the student submits, and — if the AI study assistant is used — the messages the student types to it and its replies (see §6).

Content you upload

Textbook chapters and materials teachers/parents upload, and the lessons, decks, videos, and worksheets we generate from them.

Payment information (paid plans only)

If you buy a paid plan, our payment providers (see §7) collect and process your payment details. We do not receive or store full card numbers. We store only your plan, billing status, and a provider reference needed to manage the subscription.

Technical data

To keep the service running and secure we process basic technical data such as IP address, device/browser type, and error logs. We use no advertising or analytics trackers, no behavioural-profiling SDKs, and no cookies other than the session cookie that keeps you signed in. We do not collect student phone numbers, home addresses, photos, biometrics, or precise location.

4. Children’s privacy & the school’s role

  • Student accounts exist only inside a school’s, teacher’s, or parent’s workspace and are created, named, and managed by that adult — acting with the parental consent required by applicable law (e.g. the DPDP Act, GDPR Art. 8, or COPPA’s school-consent mechanism).
  • Student data is used for exactly one purpose: delivering assigned lessons and showing the student’s own teachers, school, and parent how learning is going. We apply data minimisation — we ask for no more student data than that purpose needs.
  • We never advertise to students, never sell or share student data for commercial purposes, and never use student data (including AI-assistant conversations) to train AI models.
  • Access to student data is role-scoped: a teacher sees only their own classes; a coordinator sees only the grades assigned to them; a parent sees only their own children; leadership views of student-level data are recorded in an access log the school admin can review.
  • Parents or guardians may review, correct, or request deletion of their child’s data at any time — through the school/teacher, or directly at the contact in §1 — and may withdraw consent, which ends further processing of that child’s data.

5. How we use data & our legal bases

  • To run the service — sign-in, generating teaching materials, assigning them, the AI study assistant, and recording progress and grades for the student’s own teachers, school, and parent. Basis: performance of our contract with you (or, for student data, the school’s instructions and consent).
  • To communicate about accounts — e.g. sending login credentials to a parent’s email, password resets, and service notices. Basis: contract / legitimate interests.
  • To keep the service secure, prevent abuse, and debug failures. Basis: legitimate interests / legal obligation.
  • To take payment for paid plans. Basis: contract.

We do not use your data for advertising, and we do not sell it to anyone. Where we rely on consent (e.g. a child’s data, or premium voices), you may withdraw it at any time.

6. AI processing

SketchCast uses artificial intelligence in two ways, and we are precise about what is sent where:

  • Generating lessons. The uploaded chapter content is sent to Anthropic (Claude) to produce the lesson, deck, worksheet, and exam; the lesson script text is sent to Microsoft (Edge text-to-speech) or — only when a school enables premium voices — ElevenLabs, to produce narration. This step does not send student personal data.
  • The AI study assistant. When a teacher, parent, or student uses the optional assistant, the questions they type are sent to Anthropic (Claude), together with the relevant book/lesson content, so it can answer. This means a student’s typed messages are processed by our AI provider. A short constrained maths service (self-hosted) may also be called to check calculations. We store the assistant conversation for up to 30 days so the student can continue where they left off, and to let you review it.

Our AI providers process this content only to provide the service. Under their business terms they do not use it to train their models and are not permitted to use it for advertising. We do not use your content or your students’ data to train any AI model. The assistant is grounded in the book and is designed to give hints and method — not graded answers — but, like all AI, its output can contain errors and should be reviewed.

Some of the providers above are based in, or process data in, the United States and other countries outside your own. Where we transfer personal data across borders, we rely on appropriate safeguards — such as the European Commission’s Standard Contractual Clauses (and the UK Addendum), the providers’ own compliance commitments, and, for student data, the school’s instructions. 9. Cookies & tracking

We use a single essential cookie — the session cookie that keeps you signed in. We do not use advertising cookies, analytics cookies, cross-site trackers, or “Do Not Track”-defeating techniques. Because we use only an essential cookie, no cookie-consent banner is required, but you can clear the cookie at any time by signing out.

10. Retention & deletion

  • Account data is kept for as long as the account is active.
  • Uploaded books and generated content are kept until you delete them — teachers/parents can delete them at any time in the product.
  • Student learning records are deleted when the student is removed; removing a student deletes their profile and records with them.
  • AI-assistant conversations are kept for up to 30 days, then deleted.
  • Technical logs are kept for a short period (typically up to 90 days) for security and debugging, then deleted or anonymised.
  • To close an account or delete a school’s data entirely, email us — we complete deletion within 30 days, except where law requires longer retention (e.g. tax records of a payment). Backups are overwritten on a rolling cycle.

11. Security & breach notification

Data is encrypted in transit; passwords are hashed; every database query is scoped by row-level security so users reach only data their role permits; files are served through short-lived signed links; leadership access to student-level data is audit-logged; payment card details never touch our servers. No system is perfectly secure, but minimal collection is our first protection — what we don’t hold can’t leak.

If a personal-data breach occurs that is likely to create a risk to you, we will notify the relevant supervisory authority and affected schools/users without undue delay and within the timeframes required by applicable law, and describe what happened and what we are doing about it.

12. Your rights

Subject to your local law, you have the right to: access the personal data we hold about you; correct it; delete it; obtain a copy in a portable format; restrict or object to certain processing; and withdraw consent where we rely on it. Parents and guardians may exercise these rights for their child. We do not discriminate against you for exercising them, and we will not sell or share your data.

To exercise a right, contact us at the address in §1 — if you are part of a school, your school admin can also action most requests directly in the product. If you believe we have mishandled your data, you may lodge a complaint with your local data-protection authority (for example, the Data Protection Board of India, an EU/EEA supervisory authority, the UK ICO, or the California Privacy Protection Agency).

13. Automated processing

Some features compute helpful signals automatically — for example a “book health” quality score at upload, an at-risk learning worklist for teachers, and auto-scoring of objective quiz questions. These assist a teacher’s judgment and are always reviewable and overridable by a human; we do not make decisions producing legal or similarly significant effects about a student by automated means alone. AI-generated teaching materials should always be reviewed by a teacher before classroom use.

14. Schools: our role & a data-processing agreement

When a school uses SketchCast, the school is the controller of its students’ data and we are its processor: we process student data only on the school’s documented instructions, for the educational purpose, and under confidentiality. Schools that need a formal Data Processing Addendum (DPA) — including sub-processor terms and, where relevant, Standard Contractual Clauses — can request one at the contact in §1.
